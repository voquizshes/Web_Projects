What are planets?

Planets are large celestial bodies that orbit a star, like our Sun. They come in different sizes, colors, and compositions—some rocky, some made of gas—and each has its own unique features.

Mercury

The smallest planet and closest to the Sun, with a rocky surface and extreme temperatures.

Venus

A hot, cloudy world known for its thick atmosphere and bright appearance in our sky.

Earth

Our home planet, the only known world with life, oceans, and diverse ecosystems.

Mars

The red planet, famous for its dusty surface, huge volcanoes, and the possibility of ancient water.

Jupiter

The largest planet, a gas giant with colorful storms, including the Great Red Spot.

Saturn

A gas giant with stunning rings made of ice and rock, and dozens of moons.

Uranus

An icy giant that spins on its side, with pale blue clouds and faint rings.

Neptune

The farthest planet from the Sun, deep blue in color, with powerful winds and storms.
